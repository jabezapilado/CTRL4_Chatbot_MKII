## Core Service Statement

The University Guidance Services provides guidance and counseling support programs for students who may be experiencing distress that may affect behavior within and outside the University.

## Guidance and Counselling Areas

- Personality Development
- Relationship Concerns
- Time Management
- Stress Management
- Choice of Course
- Sexuality Education
- Academic Difficulties
- Peer Support Group

## Mental Health Program

The Mental Health Program aims to improve the psychological well-being of students.
It includes activities for mental wellness, management of mental health concerns,
psychological assessments, and referral to professionals.

## Support Populations Mentioned

- College students
- High school students
- Elementary pupils
- Parents
- Faculty
- Visitors with student-related concerns

## Guidance Highlights from Official Content

- Counselors can provide listening support for relationship and personal concerns.
- Students experiencing stress may seek guidance office support and stress management help.
- Career counseling sessions are available for students undecided about course choice.
- Academic counseling and consultation (including teachers/parents consultation) are available for students with academic difficulties.
- Peer support via Guidance Caring Connections (GCC) is part of guidance outreach.

## Safety and Scope Notes for Chatbot Grounding

- Guidance support is for counseling and student well-being, not medical diagnosis.
- Mental health concerns can involve referral to professionals.
- Crisis or emergency concerns should be escalated to proper guidance office and emergency services.
